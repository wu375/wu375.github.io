Generative learning

Generative arts, creative coding, generative coding.. These all fo some reason hint on the use of machine learning/deep learning. Actually they share one similarity. They are both, to some extent, abstract/mysterious/unexplainable. Arts are like neural networks, functions where inputs are images and outputs are, say, levels of satisfaction of viewers. It’s hard to tell what triggers different outputs, which is a digression to the ancient brain vs nn problem

But what’s important is the exact HOW: Deep learning doing creative coding/art? (Can we mimic the style of the (somewhat hard-coded) works shown above?)

This is obviously not a new thing at all. We have all those generative models like conditional gans that turn pictures into art works.

But one thing that comes to mind first is a GRN and VAE based model called Sketch-RNN, probably because I loved the concept but had procrastinated so long reading about it.

It’s interesting because it generates strokes instead of pixels.

How are strokes represented?

  • stroke-5 representation: (Δx, Δy, p1, p2, p3)
  • Δx, Δy are change from the previous point
  • p1, p2, and p3 are one hot representation of three states:
    1. touching paper (line from curr to next)
    2. lifted
    3. drawing ends
