Problem definition

Sound event detection (SED): Task to identify the type of sound event. May also identify start/end time in the long audio stream.

Like most problems, SED relies on labeling, or the start/end time locations. A concept called weak labeling exists in SED world, which refers to labels telling only types of sound events. A famous such dataset (named Audio Set) is by Google.

There is also a thing called 2017 DCASE challenge, which asked people to solve weakly labeled SED tasks.

How to predict? Easy:

  • For each audio stream, predict binary label for each sound event at local substream
  • Combine results using a pooling function

[Hello] talks about some pooling methods: Max, Average, Linear, Exp, Attention.

You can probably guess what Max and Average pooling mean. The latter three are less obvious, but just some function to map y to [0,1].
